Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

FAQ 12.1 How could new technologies to remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere contribute to climate change mitigation?

Limiting the increase in warming to well below 2°C, and achieving net zero CO 2 or GHG emissions, will require anthropogenic CO 2 removal (CDR) from the atmosphere.

The CDR methods studied so far have different removal potentials, costs, co - benefits and side effects. Some biological methods for achieving CDR, like A/R or wetland rest oration, have long been practiced. If implemented well, these practices can provide a range of co - benefits, but they can also have adverse side effects such as biodiversity loss or food price increases. Other chemical and geochemical approaches to CDR incl ude Direct Air Carbon Capture and Storage (DACCS), Enhanced Weathering or Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement. They are generally less vulnerable to reversal than biological methods.
