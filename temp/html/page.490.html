3.4.1.2 Sectoral emissions strategies and the timing of net zero Mitigation pathways show differences in the timing of decarbonization ( Figure 3 . 20 ) and the timing of net zero ( Figure 3 . 19 ) across sectors and regions ( high confidence ); t he timing in a given sector depends on the cost of abatement in it, the availability of CDR options, the scenario design, near - term emissions levels, and the amount of non - CO 2 abatement (Yeh et al. 2017; Emmerling et al. 2019; Rogelj et al. 2019a,b; Johans son et al. 2020; van Soest et al. 2021b; Ou et al. 2021; Azevedo et al. 2021) ( Cross - Chapter B ox 3 in this chapter ). However , delaying emissions reductions, or more limited emissions reductions in one sector or region, involves compensating reductions in other sectors or regions if warming is to be limited (high confidence) (Rochedo et al. 2018; Price and Keppo 2017; Grubler et al. 2018; van Soest et al. 2021b) .
