This is about the same size as the remaining carbon budget for keeping global warming to 1.5°C and between one third and half the 1150±220 ( 1350 , 1700 ) GtCO 2 for limiting global warming below 2°C with a 67% (50%, 33%) probability, respectiv ely (Canadell et al., 2021 ) . At current (2019) rates of emissions, it would only take 8 (2 - 15) and 25 (18 - 35) years to emit the equivalent amount of CO 2 for a 67 th percentile 1.5°C and 2°C remaining carbon budget, respectively. This highlights the dependence of 1.5°C pathways on the availability of substantial CO 2 removal capacities , as discussed in chapters 3, 4, and 12, but also Section 2.7 of this chapter.
