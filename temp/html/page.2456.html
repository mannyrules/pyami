14.7 Knowledge Gaps

Any assessment of the effectiveness of international cooperation is limited by the methodological

challenge of observing sufficient variance in cooperation in order to support inference on effects. There

is little in the way of cross - sectional variance, given that most of the governance mechanisms assess ed

here are global in their geographical coverage . One exception is with respect to the effects of the Kyoto

Protocol , which we have reported. Time series analysis is also challenging , given th at other

determinants of climate mitigation, including technology costs and the effects of national and sub -

national level policies, are rapidly evolving. Th us , this chapter primarily reviews scholarship that

compare s observations with theory - based counter - f actual scenarios .

Many of the international agreements and institutions discussed in this chapter, in particular the Paris

Agreement, are new. The logic and architecture of the Paris Agreement, in particular, break s new

ground , and there is limited evaluat ion of prior experience in the form of analogous treaties to draw on.
