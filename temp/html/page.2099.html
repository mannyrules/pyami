12.6.1.2 Mitigation measures from a cross - sectoral perspective

Three aspects of mitigation from a cross - sectoral perspective are considered, following (Barker et al. 2007) :

▪ mitigation measures used in more than one sector ;

▪ implications of mitigation measures for interaction and integration between sectors; and

▪ competition among sect ors for scarce resources.

A number of mitigation measures find application in more than one sector. Renewable energy technologies such as solar and wind may be used for grid electricity supply, as embedded generation in the buildings sector and for energy supply in the agriculture sector (Chapters 6, 7 and 8) (Shahsavari and Akbari 2018) . Hydrogen and fuel cells, coupled with low carbon energy technologies for producing the hydrogen, is being explored in transport, urban heat, industry and for balancing electricity supply (Chapters 6, 8, 11) (Dodds et al. 2015; Staffell et al. 2019) . Electric vehicles are considered an option for balancing variable power (Kempton and Tomić 2005; Liu and Zhong 2019) .
