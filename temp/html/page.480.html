Net zero CO 2 and carbon neutrality have different meanings in this assessment, as is the case for net zero GHG and GHG neutrality. They apply to different boundaries in the emissions and removals being considered. Net zero (GHG or CO 2 ) refers to emissions and removals under the direct control or territorial responsibility of the reporting entity. In contrast, (GHG or carbon) neutrality includes anthropogenic emissions and anthropogenic removals within and also those beyond the direct control or territorial resp onsibility of the reporting entity. At the global scale, net zero CO 2 and carbon neutrality are equivalent, as is the case for net zero GHG and GHG neutrality.
