13.2.2 National s trategies and Nationally Determined Contributions National climate strategies, which are often formulated through executive action, contribute to climate governance in several ways. Strategies enable discussion of low - emissions pathways while accounting for uncertainty, national circumstances and socio - ec onomic objectives (Falduto and Rocha 2020) . They frequently set out long term emission goals and possible trajectories over time, with analysis of technological and economic factors (Levin et al. 2018; WRI 2020) . This can include quantitative modelling of low - emissions transitions and their economic effects to inform policymakers and stakeholders of potential outcomes (Waisman et al. 2019; Weitzel et al. 2019) .
