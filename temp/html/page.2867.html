17.4 Key barriers and enablers of the transition: synthesizing results

This section provides a deep and broad synthesis of theory (section 17.2) and evidence (section 17.3) in order to identify the conditions that either enable or inhibit transitions to sustainable low - carbon futures. Following the literature on sustainabilit y transitions (see Cross - chapter Box 1 2 on Transition Dynamics ), the section finds that there is rarely a ny one single factor promoting or preventing such transition s . Rather, marked departures from business as usual typically involve several factors, incl uding technological innovations, shifts in markets, concerted efforts by scientists and civil - society organizations to raise awareness of the costs of continued emissions, social movements, policies and governance arrangements, and changes in belief system s and values.

All of this comes together in a co - evolutionary process that has unfolded globally, internationally and locally over several decades (Hansen and Nygaard 2014; Rogge et al. 2017; Sorman et al. 2020) , an d that may be guided or facilitated by interventions that target leverage points in the underlying development path (Burch and Di Bella 2021; Leventon et al. 2021) . While transitions necessarily follow context - specific trajectories, more general lessons can be drawn by compari ng the empirical details with both system - level and narrower explanations of change.
