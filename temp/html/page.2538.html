At an aggregate level, in both developed and developing countries, the vast majority of tracked climate finance is sourced from domestic or national markets rather than cross - border finan cing (Buchner et al. 2019) . This reinforces the point that national policies a nd settings remain crucial (Section 15.6.2), along with the development of local capital markets (Section 15.6.7).

Climate finance in developing countries remains heavily concentrated in a few large economies ( high confidence ), with Brazil, India, China and South Africa accounting for 25% to 43% depending on the year, a share similar to that represented by developed countries. Least - developed countries (LDCs), on the other hand, continue to represent less than 5% year - on - year (BNEF 2019; Buchner et al. 2019)
