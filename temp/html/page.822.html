Educ ating households and policy makers about the effectiveness of choice architecture and adding these behavioural tools to existing market - and regulation - based tools in a transparent and consultative way can provide desired outcomes with increased effectiven ess, while avoiding charges of manipulation or deception. People consent to choice architecture tools if their use is welfare - enhancing, policymakers are transparent about their goals and processes, public deliberation and participation is encouraged, and the choice architect is trusted (Sunstein et al. 2019) .
