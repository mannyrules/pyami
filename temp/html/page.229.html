P oli cy responses to cope with carbon leakage includ e border carbon adjustment (BCAs) and differentiated carbon tax es (Liu et al. 2020) . Some BCA options focusing on levelling the cost of carbon paid by consumers on products could be designed in line with WTO (Ismer et al. 2016) while others may not be (Mehling et al. 2019) . All proposals could involve difficulty of tracing and verifying the carbon content of inputs (Onder 2012; Denis - Ryan et al. 2016). An international consensus and certification practice on the carbon content would help to overcome WTO compatibility (Holzer 2014) .
