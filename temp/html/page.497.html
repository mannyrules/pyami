3.4.3 Buildings Global final energy use in the building sector increases in all pathways as a result of p opulation growth and increasing affluence ( Figure 3 . 24 ). There is very little difference in final energy intensity for the buildings sector across scenar ios. Direct CO2 emissions from the buildings sector vary more widely across temperature stabilization levels than energy consumption. In 2100, scenarios above 3ºC [C7 - C8] still show an increase of CO2 emissions from buildings around 29% above 2019, while a ll scenarios likely to limit warming to 2ºC and below have emission reductions of around 8 5 % (8 - 100%). Carbon intensity declines in all scenarios, but much more sharply as the warming level is reduced.
